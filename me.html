Focus Areas

Where the work is strongest.

The strongest work in this portfolio sits where exploit development, low-level reasoning, and careful debugging meet. The mix below is meant to show depth honestly rather than flatten everything into a generic “security” label.

Binary Exploitation

The primary CTF focus is pwn.

The strongest results come from challenges involving heap layout, out-of-bounds access, memory corruption, leaked state, exploit stabilization, and control-flow manipulation.

Smart Contracts

Blockchain work centers on concrete logic flaws.

That includes reentrancy, ownership mistakes, contract state assumptions, and attack paths where ordering and visibility decide whether the exploit works.

Reverse / Forensics

Cross-category work still comes from first principles.

Experience across reverse engineering and forensics helps with binary behavior, artifact triage, debugging, and building a model before choosing a solve path.

Current Growth

Web exploitation and cryptography are active growth areas.

Those categories are part of the current skill-building track, with the goal of bringing the same level of exploit discipline and technical confidence to them over time.

Working Style

How problems get approached

Most of the work here is grounded in building a reliable model first: what state exists, what invariants break, what assumptions the target makes, and how to turn that into a repeatable exploit path rather than a lucky run.

That usually means reading behavior closely, killing weak ideas quickly, and staying focused on exploitability instead of collecting disconnected observations.
